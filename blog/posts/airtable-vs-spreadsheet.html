Airtable vs Spreadsheets
for Food Business Inventory

FreeBeginner ⏱ 6 min read
◆ AIRTABLE FOR FOOD BUSINESSES     ◆ INVENTORY MANAGEMENT     ◆ SPREADSHEET ALTERNATIVE     ◆ FOOD DISTRIBUTOR TOOLS     ◆ AIRTABLE FOR FOOD BUSINESSES     ◆ INVENTORY MANAGEMENT     ◆ SPREADSHEET ALTERNATIVE    

Spreadsheets aren't bad. Excel and Google Sheets are genuinely powerful tools and plenty of small food businesses run on them just fine. The question isn't "are spreadsheets good?" — it's "is a spreadsheet the right tool for what you're trying to do?"

For inventory and product data specifically, there's a point where spreadsheets start working against you. This article helps you identify if you've reached that point.

What spreadsheets are good at

  • Quick calculations and financial modelling
  • One-off analysis and reporting
  • Data that one person maintains and controls
  • Information that doesn't need to connect to other systems
  • Getting started fast with zero cost

Where spreadsheets struggle for food business inventory

The problems usually appear gradually:

  • Multiple people editing the same file — version conflicts, accidental overwrites, "which one is the latest?"
  • The file gets emailed around — five copies exist, none of them current
  • You can't link it to anything — updating stock means updating the spreadsheet, the website, the price list, and the wholesale portal separately
  • No history or audit trail — who changed what, and when?
  • Formulas break — someone inserts a row, breaks a VLOOKUP, and no one notices until the stock count is wrong
  • You can't automate against it — you can't set a trigger to fire when stock drops below a threshold

The moment to switch

Switch from spreadsheets to Airtable when any of these apply:

  • More than one person needs to update the same data
  • You want stock data to automatically update your website or Shopify
  • You need to track allergens, nutritional data, or certifications per product
  • You want to set low-stock alerts or automated reorder reminders
  • You're planning to use AI for order processing (it needs a structured product catalogue to match against)

How to migrate without disruption

The good news: migration is straightforward. Airtable imports CSV files directly — so your existing spreadsheet becomes your starting point:

  1. Export your current spreadsheet as CSV
  2. Create a new Airtable base and import the CSV as your Products table
  3. Clean up column types (numbers as numbers, checkboxes as checkboxes)
  4. Add the fields spreadsheets can't handle: allergens, images, linked suppliers
  5. Share the base with your team and archive the spreadsheet
