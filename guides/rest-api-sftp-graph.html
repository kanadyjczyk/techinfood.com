REST API, SFTP, and Microsoft Graph:
Which Does Your Food Business Need?

Free guideBeginner ⏱ 7 min read

When people talk about connecting food business systems — your ERP to Shopify, your stock system to a customer portal, your Outlook inbox to an order workflow — they're usually talking about one of three connection methods. This guide explains each one plainly, with real food business examples.

Short answer: Most food businesses use a mix of all three, depending on the systems involved. You don't need to pick one. The method is determined by what the platform supports.

⚡
REST API
Real-time · Two-way · Modern platforms

A REST API (Application Programming Interface) lets two systems talk to each other in real time over the internet. When something happens in one system, it can instantly update another.

Think of it like a waiter in a restaurant. You (System A) tell the waiter what you want. The waiter goes to the kitchen (System B), gets what's needed, and brings it back. It happens on demand, in real time.

A Shopify order syncs to your ERP the moment it's placed
Stock levels update on your website when a product is received
Airtable product data pushes to Shopify when a record is changed
AI reads an order email and creates a draft in your system instantly
// Example: asking Airtable for a product record via REST API GET https://api.airtable.com/v0/{baseId}/Products?filterByFormula={SKU}="OOM-001" Authorization: Bearer YOUR_TOKEN Response: { "id": "recABC123", "fields": { "Product Name": "Organic Oat Milk 1L", "Price": 2.99, "Stock": 147, "Allergens": ["Oats"] }}
📁
SFTP
Scheduled · Batch · Legacy systems

SFTP (Secure File Transfer Protocol) is a way to securely move files between two systems on a schedule. Rather than real-time updates, you're typically sending a file once a day, once a week, or at another set interval.

Think of it like a courier service. You prepare a package (a file) and drop it off (upload to an SFTP server). The other party picks it up and processes it. It's not instant, but it's reliable, secure, and widely supported — especially by older systems.

Daily stock export sent to a wholesale customer's system overnight
Supplier price list received each Monday and imported into your ERP
EDI order files exchanged with a supermarket or large retailer
Sales data exported to your accountant's system weekly
Many food industry customers and suppliers still use SFTP — especially supermarkets, large retailers, and older ERP systems. Even if your own platform has a modern API, you may need SFTP to connect with their end. Both methods are relevant.
🔷
Microsoft Graph
Microsoft 365 · Office · SharePoint · Teams

Microsoft Graph is the API gateway into the entire Microsoft 365 ecosystem. If your business uses Outlook, Teams, SharePoint, Excel, or OneDrive, Microsoft Graph is how you connect other systems to them.

It's technically a REST API — but it's specific to Microsoft's platform and worth treating separately because it's so widely relevant to food businesses that run on Office 365.

Reading order emails from Outlook to feed into an AI order workflow
Creating and filing documents in SharePoint automatically
Pushing a notification to a Teams channel when a new order is approved
Reading or updating data in an Excel file on OneDrive programmatically
Sending emails or calendar invites from a system (not just manually)

Which one do you need?

Do you need data to sync in real time (the moment something changes)?
→ Use REST API. Most modern platforms — Shopify, Airtable, Foodit — support it.
Are you exchanging large batches of data on a schedule, or working with a legacy system?
→ Use SFTP. Also the right choice when a trading partner (supermarket, large retailer, older ERP) only supports file exchange.
Are you integrating with anything in Microsoft 365 — Outlook, SharePoint, Teams, Excel, OneDrive?
→ Use Microsoft Graph. It's the only way to properly integrate with these services.
