✦ How it works · Email to Sales Order
Email to Sales Order Conversion

This workflow uses AI to read incoming customer order emails and automatically create a draft sales order — eliminating the need for manual re-keying. Here's exactly how it works.

1
Email arrives in your inbox
The AI monitors a dedicated email address or your existing inbox. It works with plain text, replies, forwarded chains, and attachments.
2
AI reads and extracts the order
Natural language processing identifies product names, quantities, delivery dates, and any special instructions — even when written informally or inconsistently.
3
Lines matched to your product catalogue
The AI compares extracted product names against your catalogue using fuzzy matching — handling abbreviations, alternative names, and codes your customers use.
4
Draft sales order created for review
A complete draft order is generated and routed to you for confirmation. One click approves it; any line can be edited before it flows into your system.

What you need to implement this: An email address to monitor, your product catalogue in a structured format (Airtable works well), and a connection to your order management system or ERP. The AI layer can be built using tools like Make.com combined with an LLM API.

Quick wins

Things Most Small Food
Businesses Can Do Today

1
💡 Tip · Inventory
Replace your stock spreadsheet with a free Airtable base

Airtable's free tier supports up to 1,000 records. Create one table for Products, link it to a Suppliers table, and add a formula field to flag low stock. Takes about 2 hours to set up and immediately gives you a live, multi-user inventory view. Use our PIM guide as a starting point.

2
💡 Tip · Automation
Set up a Make.com scenario to auto-confirm customer orders by email

Make.com (free plan available) can watch a Gmail or Outlook inbox and automatically send a confirmation email when it detects a new order. Saves 5–10 minutes per order and keeps customers informed without anyone lifting a finger. Our automation guide walks through the setup.

3
💡 Tip · eCommerce
Use Shopify's built-in CSV import to bulk-load your products

If you're moving onto Shopify, you don't need to enter products one by one. Export your existing product list to CSV (Airtable can do this), map the columns to Shopify's format, and import in bulk. A 200-product catalogue can be loaded in under an hour with the right template.

4
💡 Tip · Integration
Check if your ERP or system has a REST API before paying for middleware

Most modern systems — including Airtable, Shopify, and Foodit — have free REST APIs. Before paying for an integration tool, check the documentation. Many simple connections can be built directly or with a small Make.com scenario at minimal cost. Ask us if you're unsure what your system supports.

Common questions

Questions We Get Asked

If yours isn't here, use the button below — we answer every question personally.

Do I need a developer to implement any of this?+
Not always. Airtable, Make.com, and Shopify are designed to be used without coding skills. Our guides are written for non-technical people. For more complex connections — like a custom REST API integration or an SFTP feed — some technical help is useful. We're happy to advise on whether you need it for your specific situation.
Is Airtable good enough to use as a proper PIM, or do I need dedicated software?+
For most small food businesses — especially those with up to a few hundred products — Airtable is genuinely sufficient. It handles all the core PIM requirements: structured product data, allergens, nutritional fields, pricing, imagery, and a REST API to push data to Shopify or your website. Dedicated PIM software starts at tens of thousands of pounds per year and is designed for businesses with thousands of SKUs across dozens of channels. Our Airtable PIM guide covers exactly how to set it up.
How much does AI workflow automation actually cost to implement?+
It varies. A Make.com-based email to sales order workflow using an LLM API can cost as little as £50–150/month to run once built. A more custom implementation with direct ERP integration would cost more to set up but often less to run. We're happy to give you a realistic cost estimate for your specific situation on a free call — no strings attached.
What's the difference between REST API, SFTP, and Microsoft Graph?+
REST API is for real-time data exchange between modern cloud systems — for example, syncing a Shopify order into Airtable the moment it's placed. SFTP is for scheduled, secure file transfers — for example, sending a daily stock export to a wholesale customer. Microsoft Graph is specifically for connecting to Microsoft 365 services like Outlook, SharePoint, Excel, and Teams. Most food businesses use a mix of all three depending on which systems they use. Our integration guide explains when to use each one with real food business examples.
Is this site just for food distributors, or is it useful for other food businesses too?+
All food-related businesses. Our guides are most heavily focused on distributors and wholesalers because that's where the operational complexity tends to be highest — but we cover eCommerce for food brands, inventory for farm shops and delis, systems for artisan producers, and more. If you run any kind of food-related business and need tech advice, this is for you.
