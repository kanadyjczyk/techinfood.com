Invoice Approval Automation
for Food Businesses

Free guideBeginner ⏱ 10 min read

Supplier invoices land in your inbox every day. Someone has to open each one, check it against the purchase order, check what was actually delivered, verify the amount, and then approve or query it. For a busy food distributor processing dozens of supplier invoices a week, that's hours of bottlenecked admin.

AI invoice approval automation handles the routine checking — and flags the exceptions that actually need a human eye.

What is three-way matching?

Three-way matching is the standard accounts payable check that compares three documents:

  • Purchase Order (PO) — what you agreed to buy, at what price
  • Goods Received Note (GRN) — what was actually delivered and accepted
  • Supplier Invoice — what the supplier is asking you to pay

If all three match, the invoice is clean and can be paid. If anything doesn't match — a price discrepancy, a quantity difference, a missing PO reference — it needs investigation. AI does this comparison in seconds, on every invoice, every time.

Sourdough × 48£1.80/unit48 received£1.80/unit · 48 units✓ Match
Smoked Salmon × 24£4.50/unit20 received£4.50/unit · 24 units⚠ GRN mismatch

In the example above, 2 lines auto-approve. The third line is flagged — the invoice charges for 24 units but only 20 were received. This goes to a human for review with all context attached.

How the AI workflow works

📨
Invoice arrives by email, portal, or upload
PDF, image scan, or structured file. The AI reads all formats. Supplier portals can also push invoices directly via API.
✦
AI extracts invoice data
Supplier name, invoice number, date, line items, quantities, unit prices, VAT, and total — all extracted automatically.
✦ AI step
✦
Three-way match performed
Invoice compared against the matched PO and GRN. Price tolerance checked (e.g. allow ±1%). Quantity verified. PO reference validated.
✦ AI step
⚡
Auto-approved or exception raised
Clean invoices are approved and logged. Exceptions are routed to the right person with a summary of exactly what doesn't match — so they can resolve it quickly.
💳
Payment triggered or scheduled
Approved invoices can flow directly to your accounting system (Xero, Sage, QuickBooks) or ERP for payment on the due date.

Setting your approval rules

Before implementing, decide your tolerance thresholds. Common settings for food businesses:

// Example approval rules Auto-approve if: ✓ PO reference found and matched ✓ Quantities match GRN (±0 units for fresh/chilled) ✓ Unit prices match PO (±1% tolerance for ambient) ✓ Invoice total ≤ £2,000 Flag for review if: ⚠ Price difference > 1% ⚠ Quantity on invoice > quantity received ⚠ No matching PO found ⚠ Duplicate invoice number detected ⚠ Invoice total > £2,000 (manual approval required)
Start simple. Don't try to automate everything at once. Begin with auto-approving invoices under a certain value where PO and GRN match exactly. Add more automation as you build confidence in the system.

Tools to implement this

  • Make.com — to orchestrate the workflow (watch inbox, call AI, route results)
  • An OCR + LLM combination — to extract data from PDF invoices. Tools like Mindee or a GPT-4 Vision call work well
  • Your PO and GRN data — needs to be in a queryable format (Airtable, your ERP's API, or a spreadsheet connected via Make)
  • Your accounting system — Xero, Sage, or QuickBooks all have APIs to receive approved invoices
